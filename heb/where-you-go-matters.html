Deciding Where to Go for Health Care

Sometimes it's clear where to go when you need care, such as when you're suddenly having severe chest pain or you've broken your leg. At other times, it's not so clear. It's good to learn about your choices. Knowing where to go can make a big difference in cost and time.

You have many resources to help you decide:

  • Partner Care Team - 866-432-7289. Call your dedicated Partner Care Team for more information or to find a provider.
  • 24/7 Nurseline* - 888-838-9473. The 24/7 Nurseline can also help you decide if you should call your doctor, go to the ER or treat the problem yourself.
  • If you need emergency care, call 911 or seek help from any doctor or hospital immediately.

Urgent Care Center or Free Standing ER

Knowing the Difference can Save You Money

Urgent Care Centers and Free Standing ERs can be hard to tell apart. Free Standing ERs often look a lot like Urgent Care Centers, but costs are higher, just as if you went to the ER at a hospital.

Here are some ways to know if you are at a Free Standing ER:

  • Look like Urgent Care Centers, but include EMERGENCY in facility names
  • Are open 24 hours a day, seven days a week
  • Are physically separate from a hospital
  • Are subject to the same copay as hospital ER
  • Are staffed by ER physicians

* 24/7 Nurseline is not a substitute for the medical advice of a doctor. If you have any questions or concerns regarding your health, you should discuss them with your doctor.
